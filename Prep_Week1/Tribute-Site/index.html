Biography

William James was an American philosopher and psychologist who was also trained as a physician. He was the first educator to offer a psychology course in the United States and was one of the most influential philosophers of the 19th century.



James was born on January 11, 1842, in New York City. He grew up in an intellectual environment, with his father being a philosopher and his brother, Henry James, becoming a famous writer. James studied at Harvard University and received his medical degree from the Harvard Medical School in 1869.



James taught at Harvard University for over 35 years and wrote many influential books, including The Principles of Psychology and The Varieties of Religious Experience. He was a proponent of pragmatism and believed that beliefs and ideas should be judged based on their practical value and usefulness in everyday life.
